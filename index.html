Where Low-Income Jobs are Being Lost to COVID-19
Weekly updates on the places and industries most at risk
Which neighborhoods are most vulnerable to COVID-19 job losses? To help nonprofits, foundations, and government target support to where it’s most needed, we estimated how many residents in each census tract have lost low-income jobs.
ABOUT THE DATA
Data represent low-income jobs, defined here as jobs with earnings below $40,000 per year, and do not include all jobs, such as independent contractors and those working in the gig economy. This feature does not attempt to estimate jobs with pay cuts, only job loss.
These initial estimates are based on unemployment claims from Washington state, which publishes weekly unemployment numbers by industry. To estimate the number of jobs lost by neighborhood nationwide, we apply the Washington state data on job loss by industry at all income levels to local Census tract-level data detailing the number of low-income jobs by industry.
We will update our estimates every week with new Washington state data, and then every month with national unemployment numbers from the US Bureau of Labor Statistics after the May 8 data release.
These are estimates and may not be comparable to actual job loss in each neighborhood. We highly recommend these results be interpreted as relative levels of job loss for the purposes of informing investments into neighborhoods to alleviate some of the economic burden.
